About Me

I am a Software Systems Engineer and Computer Science student at the University of South Florida with experience in both research and industry. From conducting data analysis for a 288-trial human-robot interaction study to streamlining logistics as an IT intern, I have developed a passion for extracting value from data.

My technical work now centers on the intersection of AI and data analysis, where I use deep learning and LLM-based systems to address modern engineering challenges.

Projects

Movie Review Classification (LLMs vs. RNNs)

The Motivation: As Large Language Models dominate the AI landscape, does "bigger" always mean "better" for specific tasks? This project investigates if the quantized Gemma 3 4B model can outpace traditional RNNs in sentiment accuracy.

By implementing zero-shot and chain-of-thought prompting strategies, I demonstrate how programmatic prompting can handle the "noise" of LLM outputs to achieve high-precision classification on the IMDB dataset.

Facial Recognition (ML vs. DL)

The Motivation: How much data do you really need to recognize a face? I used the Olivetti faces dataset to conduct a stress test between classical approaches (PCA and Logistic Regression) and modern deep learning baselines.

This study maps the "tipping point" where complex models begin to outperform simple ones, providing a roadmap for choosing the right architecture based on available training data.

Input-Output Analysis: US Economy

The Motivation: Can we predict the future of a global economy using linear algebra? By applying the Leontief Input-Output Model to 166 U.S. economic sectors, I built a Python-driven forecasting tool.

Using 26 years of Bureau of Economic Analysis data, this project provides a data-backed projection of the U.S. economic structure through 2033, showcasing the power of NumPy and Pandas.
